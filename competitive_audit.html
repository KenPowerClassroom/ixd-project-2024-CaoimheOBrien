Question Kahoot Typing.com Khan Academy
Engagement: How engaging is the app? Is it visually appealing? Does it have sound and animation to capture the user's attention? Does it have a good balance of challenge and reward? Kahoot! is an especially engaging in its use of gamification elements such as streaks and rewards to create a competitive enviroment for the quizes. The app has a colourful interface as well as a memorable jingle. The multi-device interface allows for multi-player game which allows for students/ players to play each other and help them to gain knowledge in their chosen topic. 7/10 The Typing.com interface is clean and has light colours to create an almost futuristic atmosphere which allows the user to engage well with the program. It is easy to navigate and intuitive and this is further proven by its use of gamification elements such as badges, streaks and points to create a competitive enviroment and motvie the users. 8/10 I think that Khan Academy is quite interactive and engaging because it uses various gamification elements such as badges and points. The app has a consitent colour theme and there isn't a lot of noise on the home screen. There isn't much to comment on as it is quite simple. 6/10
Progress tracking:How well does the app track the user's progress? Does it provide feedback and recommendations to help users improve their performance? Does it use data to personalize the learning experience? Kahoot! has a great feedback loop as the app let's you know if you have chosen the right/ wrong answer immediately after each question. It also shows you your points in comaprison to the others playing you're playing against. There is also a streak and the screen displays who has the longest streak. However, the app does not keep your progress or points for more than one session. 6/10 Typing.com has a comprehensie progess tracking system. It tells you what levels and letters that you have mastered what ones you have left to do. When completing the lessons, it gives you immediate feedback and provides personalised suggestions for the letters and letter combinations taht you are weakest in. 9/10 Khan Academy has a good progress tracking system as it allows you to see what assignments you have yet to complete and allows you to see all the assignments that oyu have completed so far. Whilst you are doing th eassignments, you are able to track your progress via the list of assignments; assignments that you have completed are marked with a greeen tick and shows you how well you have completeed each part of the assignment (i.e., the quizes show how many questions you got correct). Overall the Khan Academy ahs a good progress tracking system however, it does not generate any meaningful personalised feedback. 8/10
User experience: How user-friendly is the app? Is it easy to navigate and use? Does it have clear instructions and prompts? Does it have a well-designed interface? The Kahoot, while the home page is bright, is also very noisy and the buttons to actaully create a quiz or a join a game are hidden in the corner making it harder to engage with. The home page is filled with ads for different packages that the page offers which I feel hinders the user engagement. When actually in a game however, the interface is quite simple and intuitive with almost no training or instructions needed to figure out the gameplay. 7/10 The interface of this website is very minimalistic and clean. It is easy to maneuver and quite intuitive.The prompts are simple and comprehendible. The lessons are easily accessible and organised well in folders/ sub-folders which keep the interface clean. They are well-structured and keep the user engaged by increasing difficulty each lesson. 10/10 The interface of Khan Academy is very similar to typing.com in the sense that it was minimalistic and clean.
